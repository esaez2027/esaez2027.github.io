Digestive System

Understanding the Digestive System helps us understand how the food we eat plays a role in maintaining good health and overall well-being

Crackers and Bananas Experiment


Section 1:

Digestion Process

-The Bananas and Crackers being put in the bag simulates the
food entering the mouth

-The water represents saliva which helps break down the food

-The zip-locked bag represents the stomach

-The food crushing simulates mechanical breakdown of the
food. It also represents the churning that breaks food down
to smaller particles

-The addition of orange juice simulates the gastric juices in the stomach. Which helps in the chemical digestion of the food

-The possible biomolecules inside bananas are carbohydrates like glucose and fructose. Crackers contain protein and
carbohydrates in the form of starch.
Section 1:

Digestion Process Instructions
Subsection 1:

Transferring Process

-The liquid flowing out the stocking stimulates the nutrients
flowing to the tray representing our body

-The tray represents our body that needs the nutrients from the
food to get energy and function

-The ones left in the stocking is the things the body cannot
digest which travels to your intestines, rectum, then anus and becomes poop

-Pushing the partially solidified content simulates the waste
exiting the body through the anus by pushing the waste
through the hole
Subsection 1:

Transferring Process Instructions
Section 2:

Absorption

-The paper towel that absorbed the largest amount of water is
the one with 4 folds. This is because of its larger surface area and more space for water to be trapped and take in.

-The small intestines can be compared to the paper towels which
is responsible for absorping nutrients in our system.
Since our body needs a lot of nutrients, our small intestines
would need a large surface area for absorping like the tissue with many folds. The small intestines have villi which are
tiny finger-like projections that line the small intestines which multiplies the surface area for nutrients to be absorbed
Section 2:

Absorption Instructions
Section 3:

Bile Action

-Bile is a a mixture of fluid, electrolytes, and organic molecules produced in the liver and is stored in the gallbladder. It breaks
down and absorbs fats. Bile is involved in mechincal digestion.
This is because bile does not have any digestive enzymes for chemical digestion. Bile can be found in mechanical digestion through the emulsification of fat molecules called micelles which makes it easier for enzymes to perform during digestion.

Bile Action Instructions

CONCLUSION

The digestive system is an important part of our body crucial for our survival. We learned how different types of organs worked together in order for our body to function. Through this activity, we are able to understand how different nutrients work in our body. We can use this in the real world to be more aware of what we eat and put in our body.
